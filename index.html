Pablo Castro

Frontend developer at MOBGEN
Free software and internet freedom activist

Learner and innovative

I am always improving myself, learning new things and been updated with the last technology news.
FOSDEM, Codemotion and other conferences, talks and hackathons are in my agenda every year.

Quality focused

I am obsesed with quality and the things well done, code review, testing and continuous integration are esencial for me.
I love design patterns and good practices so I am always encouraging people around me to follow them.

Collaborative

Technology world is growing very fast thanks to the collaboration and working together.
I am a guy who is always trying to create community and focused in the team, not in myself.
If we work together we can build anything.

Skills

I started my profesional life as a Fullstack developer, working with multiple technologies as Java, Backbone or JQuery in a platform for trade logistics for the biggest textile company in the world, but my goal was to be focused in Frontend and I had the opportunity when I came to MOBGEN.
Here I am managing the frontend part of HALO, a backend as a service platform that we use to develop multiple mobile apps for very important clients with React, Redux and many other new technologies that we add every day.

Based on Galicia, best place in the world

Free software Enthusiast

Community organizer

Main organizer at GPUL, hosting from local meetups as GPUL Labs or hackathons like Hackathiño to big international conferences as GUADEC 2012 or Akademy 2015.

Speaker

Talking about freedom, GNU/Linux, licenses and free software, usually focused in React, JS and other frontend technologies.

Developer

Publishing my own free software projects as VACmatch mobile (part of VACmatch, my first startup) and with others in my mind, waiting for some free time ;).

Internet Freedom activist

I've been involved in a lot of projects and campaings last years.

I managed events in A Coruña university to protest against DRM and to stop the introduction of these digital restrictions into HTML standards.
In the Document Freedom Day, I organized talks too, trying to encourage people and public organizations to use open standards, with members of the Free Software Foundation as speakers.

At this moment privacy and internet freedom are my main worries and that's why I am contributing to some campaings like ChangeCopyright, Do not Track and many others by social networks and giving some talks to get more people involved.

And of course, I am fellow of the Free Software Foundation Europe too!
